# 样例作文原文

Lifelong learning, once regarded as a noble pursuit reserved for scholars, has become an indispensable part of modern life. In an era marked by rapid technological advancement and an ever-expanding body of knowledge, confining one's learning to the schooling years is no longer a viable option.

The necessity of continuous learning is twofold. On the one hand, skills acquired in college grow obsolete within a decade, particularly in fields such as artificial intelligence and biotechnology, which demand constant self-renewal. On the other hand, sustained learning enriches one's inner world: a retired engineer of my acquaintance, having dedicated his later years to traditional Chinese painting, testifies that cognitive vitality is preserved rather than diminished by age.

Admittedly, lifelong learning is demanding in terms of both time and energy. Yet only by embracing it as a way of life, rather than treating it as a burden, can we truly stay relevant in this volatile era. As the ancient saying aptly puts it, "Learning is like rowing a boat upstream — not to advance is to drop back."

（约 178 词）
